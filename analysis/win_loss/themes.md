## Win Themes

### Why We Win: Time to Value

**Pattern**: Prospects choose us because they can get up and running in hours, not weeks.

**Frequency**: Mentioned in 65% of won deal transcripts

**Evidence**:
- Account_023: "We evaluated 3 tools. You were the only one where we got value on day 1."
- Account_041: "Our eng team is lean. We can't spend 2 weeks onboarding a new tool."
- Account_067: "Tried it Friday afternoon, deployed to prod on Monday. That sold us."

**How to Amplify**:
- Lead messaging with "Live in 1 hour" vs. competitors' "2 week implementation"
- Offer guided onboarding calls for hesitant prospects
- Case studies showing fast time-to-value

---

### Why We Win: No DevOps Team Required

**Pattern**: Mid-market companies don't have dedicated SRE/DevOps, our self-service model fits.

**Frequency**: Mentioned in 55% of won deals

**Evidence**:
- Account_089: "We're 30 engineers. Can't dedicate someone to DevOps full-time."
- Account_102: "Need something that eng team can use without specialized knowledge."

**How to Amplify**:
- Position against "enterprise" tools that need dedicated teams
- Show case studies with lean eng teams (10-50 people)
- Free trial lets them prove it themselves

---

### Why We Win: Pricing Model Aligns with Usage

**Pattern**: Per-project pricing (not per-seat) resonates with how they think about cost.

**Frequency**: Mentioned in 40% of won deals

**Evidence**:
- Account_115: "Other tools charge per seat. With 50 engineers, that's $$$. Your model makes sense."
- Account_134: "We have 10 projects, not 100. Pay for what we use."

**How to Amplify**:
- Lead with pricing structure in discovery
- ROI calculator comparing seat-based vs. project-based

## Loss Themes

### Why We Lose: Building In-House

**Pattern**: Larger teams (>50 eng) decide to build internal tool instead.

**Frequency**: 30% of lost deals

**Evidence**:
- Account_156: "Our CTO wants to build this internally. We have the capacity."
- Account_178: "Decided to dedicate 2 engineers to building our own solution."

**When This Happens**:
- Larger companies (>500 employees)
- Strong internal tools culture
- Have bandwidth (not shipping fast)

**How to Counter**:
- Emphasize maintenance burden: "Who owns this in 2 years when those engineers leave?"
- Share stories of companies that regretted building in-house
- Position as "buy your way out of a distraction"

**Should We Fight This?**:
- If they're >1000 employees with dedicated platform team: Probably not our ICP
- If they're 50-200 employees: Yes, revisit in 6 months when maintenance hurts

---

### Why We Lose: "Too Expensive"

**Pattern**: Sticker shock, especially for companies coming from open source or free tiers.

**Frequency**: 20% of lost deals

**Evidence**:
- Account_189: "Love the product but can't justify $30K right now."
- Account_201: "We're using GitHub Actions for free. Hard to switch to paid tool."

**When This Happens**:
- SMB/early-stage (Seed, pre-revenue)
- Haven't had painful incident yet (cost is abstract)
- Economic buyer not engaged (champion can't get budget)

**How to Counter**:
- Get economic buyer involved early (don't spend time with championif they can't get budget)
- Frame cost vs. engineer time: "$30K = 150 hours of engineer time saved"
- Offer smaller starting package or pilot

**Should We Fight This?**:
- If they're <10 employees, <$1M revenue: Not our ICP, let them churn from free tier later
- If they're 50+ employees, >$5M revenue: Yes, it's objection handling not true price issue

---

### Why We Lose: Prefer Competitor X

**Pattern**: Already invested in competitor's ecosystem, hard to switch.

**Frequency**: 15% of lost deals

**Evidence**:
- Account_212: "We're already using [Competitor] for monitoring. Easier to add their deploy tool."
- Account_234: "Our team is trained on [Competitor]. Switching cost is high."

**When This Happens**:
- Existing relationship with competitor (e.g., using their monitoring tool)
- Large team (retraining cost matters)
- Multi-year contract (locked in)

**How to Counter**:
- Emphasize integration: "We work alongside [Competitor], not instead of"
- Highlight where we're better: "Their monitoring is great, but deployment is not their core focus"
- Wait for renewal window

**Should We Fight This?**:
- If just renewed (1-2 years left): No, wait for renewal
- If unhappy with competitor: Yes, worth switching cost

## How to Use This Data

### For Product Team:
- **Build what helps you win**: Fast onboarding, self-service
- **Address loss reasons**: Comparison tools, competitive feature parity

### For Marketing:
- **Amplify win themes**: Case studies, messaging on "no DevOps team needed"
- **Counter loss themes**: Comparison pages, "buy vs. build" content

### For Sales:
- **Qualify out early**: If they're building in-house and won't budge, disengage
- **Get economic buyer early**: Champion without budget = time wasted
